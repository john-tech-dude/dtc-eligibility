Transfer Agents & the Issuer–DTC Interface

FAST, DWAC, DRS, and the day-to-day job of moving shares between issuer books and Cede & Co.
Core · Issuer Ops
Home › Documents › Transfer Agent Operations
Teaching focus: the transfer agent as protagonist at the issuer–DTC boundary. Pair with the eligibility questionnaire (why FAST matters for eligibility) and the certificated deposit lifecycle (paper into Cede).

This guide answers one question: who keeps the issuer’s register accurate when nearly all public float sits in the name of Cede & Co., and how do shares move between that register and DTC without a truck full of certificates? Eligibility explains whether an issue may sit at DTC. This module explains how the transfer agent runs the pipe.

The transfer agent (TA) maintains the issuer’s official list of registered holders, processes transfers, handles certificates when they still exist, and—under DTC’s FAST program—adjusts electronic balances so Cede’s position and the TA’s books stay reconciled. Broker participants face DTC; investors face brokers; the TA faces the issuer’s books and the DTC interface.

FAST — Fast Automated Securities Transfer

FAST is DTC’s program that lets an enrolled transfer agent keep a balance certificate (or electronic equivalent) registered to Cede & Co. representing the aggregate DTC-held position. Daily activity becomes balance adjustments between the TA and DTC—not a new jumbo certificate for every deposit or withdrawal.

Without FAST, many movements still require physical presentment and reregistration (see the certificated deposit lifecycle). With FAST, the issuer side of DTC eligibility becomes operationally feasible at modern volumes.

Concept Map — Issuer–TA–DTC Interface

Issuer oversees transfer agent; transfer agent maintains register and FAST balance with DTC and Cede and Co; participants hold street name; DRS holders sit on TA books in investor name. DWAC connects participants and the transfer agent.

FAST keeps Cede’s bulk position synchronized with the TA. DRS uses the same TA books with the investor’s name—not Cede’s—on the line. DWAC is the instruction path between participant and TA.

Transfer agent hub DTC / Cede (FAST) DRS holders DWAC instruction path
Eligibility link: DTC eligibility materials expect a FAST-capable TA (or equivalent arrangements). See the eligibility teaching form and Operational Arrangements—not just a checklist item on a PDF.
  • Balance certificate: represents the aggregate DTC position in Cede’s name on the TA’s books.
  • Daily reconciliation: TA and DTC must agree on the FAST balance; breaks are operational incidents.
  • Not “DTC owns the company”: Cede is nominee holder of record for deposited shares; economic owners remain beneficial holders through participants.

DWAC — Deposit / Withdrawal at Custodian

DWAC is the electronic instruction path for depositing securities into DTC (increasing Cede’s FAST balance) or withdrawing them (decreasing Cede’s balance, often into DRS or a certificated/registered form). The participant initiates; the TA approves or rejects against the register, restrictions, and documentation.

Process Map — DWAC Deposit

Electronic deposit into DTC under FAST. Six steps from eligible register position to street-name credit. Withdrawal reverses the balance direction.

1–2 · Instruct 3–4 · TA review 5–6 · Book & credit
  1. 1

    Eligible position ready

    Shares sit on the TA register (e.g. DRS or free registered) without blocking stops, freezes, or legends that prevent free deposit.

    Register · clean status
  2. 2

    Participant DWAC deposit instruction

    Broker or bank submits a deposit request for CUSIP and quantity into DTC—timing, quantity, and account must match the register.

    Participant → DTC / TA channel
  3. 3

    TA review

    Match holder, quantity, authority/medallion if needed, stop-transfer checks, legend status, and any Rule 144 / opinion package.

    Legal + ops gate
  4. 4

    Approve or reject

    Reject returns a reason to the participant (docs, legend, mismatch). Approve commits the books and proceeds to FAST adjustment.

    Decision · audit trail
  5. 5

    FAST balance ↑ to Cede

    TA and DTC adjust the balance certificate; Cede’s registered bulk on the issuer books increases by the deposited quantity.

    Electronic balance · no jumbo print
  6. 6

    Participant credits customer

    Street-name inventory posts to the customer (subject to firm holds). Shares can enter the settlement path for trading.

    Street name · settle-ready

A DWAC withdrawal reverses the balance: participant instruction → TA approval → FAST balance ↓ → shares land in DRS or other registered form.

Concept Map — FAST Balance Moves

Deposit increases Cede FAST balance and street name; withdrawal decreases Cede FAST balance and increases DRS or registered holdings.

Withdrawal

Used for direct registration, gifting, estate, or preference to hold on issuer books. Trading speed usually falls until shares return via deposit.

DRS vs Street Name — Decision Tree

DRS (Direct Registration System) holds book-entry shares on the TA’s register in the investor’s (or entity’s) name—not through a broker’s DTC participant account. It is not a paper certificate; it is not street name. See also the DTC guide’s Street Name vs DRS map.

Street name path

Investor → Participant → DTC / Cede → TA register

Fast trading and settlement. Issuer books show Cede as holder. Communications and votes ride the intermediary stack.

DRS path

Investor → Transfer agent → Named on register

Direct registration on issuer/TA books. Selling usually requires a DWAC deposit back into street name first—plan lead time under T+1.

Concept Map — Two Paths, One Register

Split diagram: street name routes investor through participant and Cede on the left; DRS routes investor straight to transfer agent books on the right; both connect to the issuer register.

Both paths end in the same TA register. Street name lines the register with Cede; DRS lines it with the investor. Liquidity lives almost entirely on the street path.

Issuer counsel tip: DRS does not remove securities law restrictions. A legended restricted share on DRS is still restricted—the holding form changed, not the exemption path.

Restricted Stock, Legends & Documentation

Most TA rejects on DWAC deposits are not “DTC is down.” They are register, authority, or restriction failures.

  1. Legend mismatch Certificate or book-entry notation still shows restricted legend while the participant requests free deposit without a removal package.
  2. Rule 144 / exemption package incomplete Missing seller’s representation, broker’s representation, legal opinion, or Form 144 when required—TA will not free the shares.
  3. Stop-transfer / freeze Court order, lost certificate stop, compliance hold, or issuer instruction blocks movement until cleared.
  4. Medallion / authority failure Endorsement, POA, corporate resolution, or medallion guarantee insufficient for the transfer type.
  5. Quantity or CUSIP mismatch Instruction does not match the registered position, class, or CUSIP (including post-split identifiers).

For private / Rule 144A / Reg S context at the entity level, see Corporate Structures. For “who owns the claim once it is at DTC,” see ownership stack and UCC Article 8.

Common Fail Points (Ops Checklist)

  • Assuming FAST means automatic free trading — FAST moves balances; legends and exemptions still govern.
  • Confusing TA reject with broker error only — read the reject reason; many are legal/docs, not “try again.”
  • DRS for a sale tomorrow — build in DWAC deposit lead time under T+1.
  • Wrong fiduciary title on DRS — same trust-titling risks as any register error.
  • Ignoring corporate actions mid-move — record dates and CUSIP changes during a pending DWAC create breaks; coordinate with CA desks.
  • Reconciliation neglect — FAST balance breaks between TA and DTC are priority incidents, not “IT noise.”

Scenario-Based Learning

Work each as TA ops, issuer counsel, or broker CA/transfer desk.

Scenario A — DWAC deposit rejected

A broker submits a DWAC deposit for 50,000 shares. The TA rejects: “Restricted legend; no opinion package.” The client says the holding period is “long enough” under Rule 144.

Questions:

  • What does the TA need before approving free deposit?
  • Who assembles the package—issuer counsel, selling shareholder counsel, or broker?
  • Does FAST enrollment change the legal standard for legend removal?
Key takeaway: FAST/DWAC are pipes. Legend removal is a securities-law and register decision, not a settlement-system override.

Scenario B — “I’ll just DRS it and sell Friday”

An investor holds free shares in street name, withdraws by DWAC to DRS “for safety,” then instructs a sale for Friday settlement under T+1 without redepositing.

Questions:

  • Can the broker settle that sale from DRS inventory?
  • What sequence restores street-name position?
  • How would you explain the trade-off in one sentence to a retail client?
Key takeaway: DRS improves direct registration; it does not replace DTC inventory for exchange settlement. Plan the DWAC deposit before the trade.

Scenario C — FAST balance out of balance

DTC shows a Cede FAST balance 1,200 shares higher than the TA’s balance certificate for the same CUSIP after a busy earnings week with splits-related adjustments on a related class.

Questions:

  • Why is this a priority for both TA and DTC?
  • Which teams must coordinate (CA, transfer, participant services)?
  • What investor-facing symptoms might appear before the break is fixed?
Key takeaway: The FAST balance is the operational truth link between issuer books and the depository. Breaks threaten wrong allocations and failed movements.
