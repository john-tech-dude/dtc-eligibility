Editorial Note on Transliteration: The rendering of the English letter "C" as כ (Kaf, value 20) rather than ק (Qof, value 100) is a deliberate theological choice, not a philological claim. The theological aptness of Kaf — "the palm that grasps" — as a depository is independently compelling, which is why the choice is defensible; it is not, however, uniquely determined.
