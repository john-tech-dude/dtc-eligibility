Self-Paced Teaching Guides

One idea, followed through eight kinds of paper: ownership isn't real until it's recorded

Every document in this collection — a securities questionnaire, a bill of exchange, a federal surety bond, a trust instrument — answers the same underlying question: who owns what, and who can prove it? This site traces that question through the U.S. book-entry settlement system operated by DTC and its nominee, Cede & Co., then follows it outward into trade finance, government contracting, non-profit endowments, trust structures, corporate ownership, and collateralized lending. Each guide pairs plain-language explanations with clickable concept maps, sourced definitions, and a short self-graded quiz.

Settlement Cycle Evolution

The U.S. securities industry has progressively shortened settlement cycles to reduce counterparty risk, improve capital efficiency, and enhance market resilience. The current T+1 standard took effect May 28, 2024.

1993
T+3 Standard SEC establishes three-business-day settlement
2017
T+2 Shortened to two business days for most securities
2024
T+1 U.S., Canada & Mexico move to one-day settlement
2027
T+1 EU, UK & Switzerland align to T+1 on 11 Oct 2027
Future
T+0 Atomic settlement via DLT and real-time gross settlement

Post-Trade Infrastructure at a Glance

The U.S. settlement ecosystem is governed by DTCC and its subsidiaries, designated as Systemically Important Financial Market Utilities (SIFMUs) under Dodd-Frank. Cross-border settlement operates through the Euroclear-Clearstream Bridge and links to local CSDs.

DTC

The Depository Trust Company — the world's largest central securities depository. Holds securities in book-entry form through its nominee, Cede & Co. Industry-owned, regulated by the SEC.

SIFMU · CSD · Book-entry

NSCC

National Securities Clearing Corporation. Provides centralized clearing, risk management, and netting for equities and corporate debt. Operates the Continuous Net Settlement (CNS) system.

CCP · Netting · Equities

FICC

Fixed Income Clearing Corporation. Clears and settles U.S. government securities, mortgage-backed securities, and repo transactions through the GSD and MBSD divisions.

Repo · Treasuries · MBS

Euroclear

International central securities depository (ICSD) based in Brussels. Settles cross-border trades via the Bridge to Clearstream and links to 50+ local CSDs. Handles Eurobonds and domestic securities.

ICSD · Bridge · Cross-border

Cede & Co.

DTC's partnership nominee. All securities deposited at DTC are registered in the name of Cede & Co. The issuer and trustee know only this nominee; beneficial ownership exists solely on intermediary books.

Nominee · Record holder · DTC

T+1 Settlement

Effective May 28, 2024, the U.S. shortened the standard settlement cycle from T+2 to T+1 for most broker-dealer transactions. Canada and Mexico aligned on May 27, 2024. EU/UK target October 2027.

SEC Rule 15c6-1 · Risk reduction
